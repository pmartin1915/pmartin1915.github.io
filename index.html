BoardBound

On the App Store

Clinical education · Martin Apps LLC flagship

Say it out loudAn AI tutor that preps nurse practitioner students for their board exams. It explains clinical reasoning the way a real preceptor would.

Studying for NP boards mostly means grinding through question banks alone. BoardBound pairs a 500+ question bank, tagged to both the AANP and ANCC exam blueprints, with a live AI tutor (built on Claude) that walks through why an answer is right. The tutor is grounded strictly in the human-written rationale attached to each question, so it never invents clinical facts. Adaptive spaced repetition, a readiness dashboard, and a Deep Dive mode for multi-system reasoning round it out. It's a real business: LLC formed, pricing set at $29.99/month, with a long-term path toward institutional licensing to nursing schools.

Burn Wizard

On the App Store

Clinical education · Martin Apps LLC · free

Say it out loudI work in a pediatric burn unit, so I built the app I wished students had. It teaches burn assessment and fluid resuscitation on an interactive body diagram.

Burn Wizard teaches the three core calculations of burn care on an interactive, age-adjusted body diagram: the Lund-Browder method for body surface area, the Parkland formula for fluid resuscitation, and Holliday-Segar maintenance fluids. An 8-chapter curriculum runs from pathophysiology through long-term management, with randomized trainer scenarios and clinical documentation templates. Fully translated into Latin-American Spanish, fully offline, and deliberately positioned as an educational tool rather than a clinical device.

By the numbers1,700+ automated tests · 8-chapter curriculum · English + Spanish · live on iOS and web

OPG Tumor Segmentation

IRB-approved research

Doctoral research · DNP scholarly project · UAB / Children's of Alabama

Say it out loudI'm training a deep-learning model that measures brain tumors in kids with NF1 automatically, in 3D, from their MRI scans. Today that measurement is still done by hand.

Children with Neurofibromatosis Type 1 develop optic pathway gliomas whose growth is tracked by hand-measured MRI, a process that is slow and inconsistent between readers. As co-investigator, I lead development of an nnU-Net deep-learning pipeline for automated 3D volumetric measurement, training on UAB's Cheaha supercomputer with NVIDIA A100 GPUs. I recruited the pediatric neuro-oncology and neuroradiology faculty team, authored a scoping review of 28 published studies now in revision at the Journal of Neuro-Oncology, and carried the project through IRB approval. Part of the UAB/Children's of Alabama Clinical Scholars Program.

Pediatric ED De-Escalation Protocol

Presented June 2026

Doctoral research · DNP quality-improvement capstone (team of 3)

Say it out loudOur team built a standardized de-escalation protocol for a pediatric psychiatric ER, so the response to a child in crisis depends on the protocol rather than on who happens to be working that shift.

The pediatric psychiatric emergency unit at a large children's hospital had no standardized protocol for behavioral crises. Responses varied by individual experience, driving restraint use and staff injuries. Using the PDSA framework, our three-student DNP team ran the full pre-implementation arc: a gap analysis combining incident-report data, a 12-item staff survey, and a policy review against best-practice benchmarks; then a seven-section tiered protocol spanning early recognition through post-incident debriefing, aligned with trauma-informed care; then a phased implementation plan with simulation-based training and a metrics-based evaluation framework. Presented at a national nursing education dissemination event in June 2026.

By the numbers7-section tiered protocol · 3 baseline data sources · published evidence shows structured protocols cut restraint use by 27 to 76 percent

PDSA framework · gap analysis · survey design · evaluation metrics

ECG Wizard

Working prototype

Clinical education · personal project

Say it out loudA web app that reads real hospital-grade ECG waveforms and classifies the rhythm, built on the same public datasets researchers use.

An installable web app that analyzes genuine 500 Hz ECG recordings from verified public medical datasets (WFDB format) rather than synthetic textbook strips. A Python signal-processing backend extracts twelve features covering rate, rhythm, and waveform morphology, then classifies normal sinus rhythm, atrial fibrillation, and myocardial infarction, with an interactive waveform dashboard on the front end. Built to learn the full clinical ML stack end-to-end: raw signal in, explainable classification out.

By the numbers500 Hz medical-grade signal data · 12 extracted features · 3 validated rhythm classes

React · TypeScript · Plotly · Python · FastAPI · scikit-learn

Land & Auction Analyzer

In personal use

Personal tools · real-estate analysis

Say it out loudIt reads the state's entire tax-auction list of about two thousand properties and ranks every parcel by investment value before I've had my coffee.

Alabama's Department of Revenue auctions tax-delinquent land, but the listings are built for bureaucrats, not buyers. This system scrapes the full list (~2,000 parcels), validates and enriches each property, scores them on metrics like price per acre, and exports a ranked sheet, with scrapers extending to Arkansas, Texas, and Florida. A companion analysis app layers on portfolio analytics and a prediction engine behind a REST API. The project that proves the skills generalize beyond healthcare.
